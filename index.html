Sachchidanand Soaham Gupta

PhD Candidate in Chemistry

My research lies at the intersection of nanomaterials chemistry, chirality control, and bioanalytical applications, with a primary focus on single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) as tunable near-infrared (nIR) optical platforms.

During my doctoral work at the University of Pittsburgh, I investigate methods to precisely control and exploit the unique optical and chiroptical properties of SWCNTs. My research combines non-covalent enantiomer separation, sp³-defect engineering, and optical chirality modulation, aiming to create next-generation SWCNT-based probes for biosensing and biointerface design.

Research Interests

Nanomaterials & Chirality

Exploring chirality in low-dimensional nanomaterials and developing novel defect-engineering strategies to control their optical and chiroptical properties.

SWCNT Platform Engineering

Using DNA-wrapping and chemical functionalization to create tailored single-walled carbon nanotube (SWCNT) platforms with modulated photoluminescence.

Bioanalytical Sensing

Designing sensitive and selective near-infrared (nIR) fluorescent probes for detecting biomolecules and studying biochemical interactions at nano-bio interfaces.
