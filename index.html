Sachchidanand Soaham Gupta

PhD Candidate in Chemistry, University of Pittsburgh

I am a doctoral researcher in the Department of Chemistry at the University of Pittsburgh, where I have been working since 2022. I work in Prof. Alexander Star's Sensor Technology Advanced Research (STAR) group focusing on nanomaterials and bioanalytical chemistry. I received my M.Sc. in Applied Chemistry from Amity University, Noida, and my B.Sc. in Industrial Chemistry from the University of Delhi.

My research interests lie at the intersection of nanomaterials chemistry, chirality control, and bioanalytical applications, with a primary focus on harnessing single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) as tunable near-infrared (nIR) optical platforms. My long-term goal is to develop precise methods to control and exploit the unique optical and chiroptical properties of SWCNTs for advanced biosensing and biointerface engineering.

In pursuit of this vision, my research explores the following key themes:

  • Applications in biosensing and biointerface design

Research Interests

Nanomaterials & Chirality

Exploring chirality in SWCNTs and exploring novel defect-engineering strategies to control their optical and chiroptical properties.

SWCNT Platform Engineering

Using DNA-wrapping and surfactant ATPE to perform entnatiomeric enrichment of SWCNTs and use chemical functionalization to create tailored single-walled carbon nanotube (SWCNT) platforms with modulated photoluminescence..

Bioanalytical Sensing

Designing sensitive and selective near-infrared (nIR) fluorescent probes for detecting biomolecules and studying biochemical interactions at nano-bio interfaces.
